I am a theoretical physicist working at the intersection of quantum information science and fundamental physics. I currently hold a Surrey Future Fellowship.

Current research

Measurements allow us to interrogate nature. Progress in the foundations of physics therefore requires a theory of measurements suitable for regimes where fundamental physics becomes relevant. Vision

My professional practice is guided by the following ideas:

  • Understanding time is one of the most important tasks of science.
  • To gain deeper insight into the foundations of physics, an effective approach is to address inconsistencies in regimes where conflicting physical theories overlap.
  • By extending propositional calculus, probability theory provides the best framework to connect physical theories with experiments.
  • Science papers should not only inform, but also teach.

What does a theoretical physicist do?

My job is to search for the general principles that govern nature and to create mathematical frameworks for their precise formulation. My motivation is to identify rational explanations for the phenomena that shape human experience. Creating and understanding are therefore my core activities.
